# Voice for AI/ML release posts

Practitioners are saturated with launch hype. Earn attention by being specific.

## Do
- Lead with the *concrete* novelty: a number, a capability, a license change, a constraint solved. "First Apache-2.0 72B model to clear MMLU 80." beats "Exciting new release!".
- Tell people what to compare it to. New models exist relative to other models. Mention the closest reference point.
- Quote one thing from the model card / abstract verbatim if it's striking.
- Acknowledge what's *not* known. "Authors haven't published an eval suite yet" is more useful than glossing over it.

## Don't
- Don't say "groundbreaking", "revolutionary", "game-changing", "state-of-the-art" without a number.
- Don't use "🚀", "🔥", "🤯" unless you'd say it aloud at dinner.
- Don't pad with hashtags. One topic hashtag, max two.
- Don't speculate about benchmarks the authors haven't reported.
- Don't write the same post for a Llama fine-tune as for a from-scratch architecture release.

## Length

Under 600 characters for the hook + body if it's going to a microblog. Longer
is fine for Discord / newsletter formats. Cut anything that doesn't move the
reader's understanding forward.
